## Headline

OpenAI announces GPT-5 at 10x the cost of GPT-4. Meanwhile, Anthropic drops Claude prices by 60%. The AI pricing war has officially begun.

## Context

The AI industry is at a crossroads. OpenAI just dropped GPT-5 with capabilities that dwarf GPT-4—but at a premium price point that has startups sweating. Simultaneously, Anthropic is playing the long game, slashing prices to capture market share.

Here's what's happening:
- **GPT-5 Pro**: $200/month for unlimited access
- **Claude 4 Opus**: Now 60% cheaper than GPT-4
- **Google Gemini**: Bundled free with Workspace
- **xAI Grok**: Still trying to find its footing

The big question: Is AI becoming a commodity, or is quality worth the premium?

## Plot Twist

Here's what nobody's talking about: The real winner might not be OpenAI or Anthropic. It's the API wrapper companies that are quietly undercutting everyone.

These middlemen bundle multiple models, offer unified APIs, and undercut official prices by 40-60%. They're capturing the SMB market that neither OpenAI nor Anthropic wants to touch.

The pricing war isn't between AI giants—it's between AI giants and the API layer. And the API layer is winning.
