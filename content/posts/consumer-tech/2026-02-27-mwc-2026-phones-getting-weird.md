The Ver headline this morning: "Phones are going to get weird next week." They're not wrong. MWC 2026 is showing foldable gaming handhelds, AI-powered everything, and designs that make you do a double-take.

And honestly? I'm here for it.

## The Smartphone Rut

Smartphones have been boring for years. Same slab of glass. Slightly better camera. Incrementally faster chip. We've hit a plateau where the $400 phone does everything the $1400 phone does, just with fewer cameras you'll never use.

Samsung's S26? It's fine. It's good, even. But it's not *different*. It's the same thing, version 14.

## Enter The Weird

MWC is bringing the chaos:

- **Foldable gaming handhelds** — Phones that unfold into mini Nintendo Switches. Will anyone buy them? Probably not. Do I want to try one? Absolutely.
- **AI hardware** — Dedicated AI chips, neural processing units, "agentic" features that feel half-baked but signal where this is going
- **Unconventional form factors** — Rollable screens, dual-hinged disasters, things that shouldn't exist

## Why This Matters

The smartphone industry needs a kick. Apple's doing AR glasses (maybe). Samsung's playing it safe. Chinese brands are actually shipping weird stuff.

When phones get weird, companies take risks. When they take risks, someone eventually figures out what actually works. Remember how foldables were a joke three years ago? Now they're just another option.

## My Take

I don't know if any of these MWC devices are good. Most will probably be bad. But the fact that someone's willing to ship something genuinely different — not just "iPhone but cheaper" — matters.

We've had a decade of iteration. Time for some experimentation.

## What I'm Watching

- Which of these weird form factors actually solves a real problem
- Whether "AI hardware" becomes meaningful or just marketing
- If the budget weird stuff (looking at you, Nothing Phone competitors) forces Samsung/Apple to actually innovate

The Galaxy S26 isn't interesting. But whatever weird foldable handheld comes out of this conference? That might be.
